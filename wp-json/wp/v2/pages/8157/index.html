Disc Injury Treatment in State College<\/h3>\n

The little pads that lie between your vertebrae are called the intervertebral discs. Each of these discs has a tough outer ring and a soft gel-like center. Your disc’s job is to both join and to separate your vertebrae from one another. These discs act as cushions, or shock absorbers, so that the bones don\u2019t make contact with each other as you move around, carry large objects, and bend over. Without the discs you would literally be unable to move your upper torso. Here are a few of the different kinds of disc injuries that make movements of the upper torso painful and difficult:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • A protruding disc occurs when the disc becomes asymmetrical and the gel is pushed out or bulging.<\/li>\n
  • A herniated disc<\/a>\u00a0is when the disc material has ruptured to the point that the gel pushes outward.<\/li>\n
  • A disc extrusion is the worst type of disc injury. This is when the gel balloons extensively into the area outside the vertebrae or breaks off from the disc.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
