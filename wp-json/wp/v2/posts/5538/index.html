How many times have we all heard the same story?\u00a0 Someone resolves to \u201cget in shape”.\u00a0 They buy the gear, the shoes, the gym membership.\u00a0 They head to the gym one, two, or several more times.\u00a0 They have grandiose dreams of a perfect body and want it after the first few trips.<\/p>\n

The reality of the matter is they fail to properly prepare for the exercise program, they over do it the first few trips, they end up being sore for days or self inflicting an injury causing even longer delays and potential medical bills.\u00a0 The goal of beginning an exercise program and getting into shape is often never realized because those that have set the goal are trying to rush the process, exercising harder and not smarter.<\/p>\n

How To Exercise Smarter<\/h3>\n

So what does exercising smarter entail?\u00a0 That is a simple answer, TAKE IT SLOW!\u00a0 If you have not been exercising your body is capable of doing only a little bit of exercise before it becomes strained and sore. Additionally, even if you have been exercising for some time, you should not increase the intensity of your workout drastically because you risk the same soreness and potential injury.\u00a0 Rushing yourself will most likely result in injury and keep you from slow steady progress that will lead to you reaching your goals.<\/p>\n

Exercise for Life<\/h3>\n

The smartest exercise strategy is slow, gradual and long-term, depending upon your age and current fitness level.\u00a0 It may be wise to begin with a walking program, gradually increasing your pace and length of the walk over time.\u00a0 An exercise program should not be something you tackle for a few weeks or months.\u00a0 It should be a lifelong healthy habit.<\/p>\n

The same slow, gradual approach should be used regardless of the type of exercise you are performing.\u00a0 If you are using weights, doing pilates or yoga, it is wise to take it very slow at first.\u00a0\u00a0 Always listen to what your body is telling you, less is more in the long run.\u00a0 If you are consistent you will begin to notice amazing results like increased energy levels, better posture and flexibility, healthier complexion, and a more restful sleep.<\/p>\n
